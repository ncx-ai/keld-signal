1. WHAT THE SOFTWARE DOES

The Software configures supported AI coding tools on your computer (such
as Claude Code, OpenAI Codex, and Gemini CLI) to report usage telemetry to
your organization's Keld Atlas service, and runs a local background
service that analyzes your AI-tool usage ON YOUR COMPUTER to produce
derived, privacy-preserving signal for your organization. The Software is
designed around one invariant: YOUR PROMPTS, YOUR CODE, AND THE AI'S
RESPONSES ARE NEVER TRANSMITTED OFF YOUR COMPUTER.

3. USAGE TELEMETRY THE SOFTWARE COLLECTS

Once configured, your AI coding tools and the Software report the
following usage metadata to Keld Atlas:

  (a) the AI model used and per-request token counts (input, output,
      cache read, and cache creation);
  (b) notional cost — a list-price equivalent of the tokens consumed,
      provided for reporting only; it is not a charge, an invoice, or a
      statement of amounts owed (in particular, not on flat-rate plans);
  (c) session identifier, request identifier, and request duration;
  (d) your work email address, terminal type, and the version of the AI
      tool in use (e.g., Claude Code, Codex, or Gemini CLI version);
  (e) the event type of each recorded event (for example "api_request"
      or a tool-call event).

4. ON-DEVICE ANALYSIS (ENRICHMENT)

The keld-agent background service reads the local transcripts of your AI
coding sessions ON YOUR COMPUTER and derives, per prompt, classification
labels such as: task type, subject-matter domain, activity type, a
work/personal indication, an inferred business function and subcategory,
a sensitivity category, and the prompt's length in characters. Sensitive
content detected in a prompt (such as email addresses, credentials, API
keys, or government identifiers) is MASKED on your computer before
anything is transmitted; masking of sensitive content is always on and
cannot be disabled by you, your organization, or Keld. Only the derived
labels and masked span placeholders are transmitted to Keld Atlas.

Your organization's administrator may enable an optional setting
("include_entity_text", off by default) under which short, non-sensitive
subject-matter terms appearing in prompts (for example, the name of a
technology or product being worked on) are included with the derived
labels. Sensitive content remains masked regardless of this setting.

5. WHAT IS NEVER COLLECTED

The Software does not transmit: your prompt text; your source code or
file contents; the AI's responses; or the unmasked text of any content
classified as sensitive. Transcripts are read locally, analyzed locally,
and never uploaded.

6. LOCAL FILE ACCESS AND CONFIGURATION CHANGES

To perform the functions above, the Software: (a) modifies the
configuration files of your AI coding tools to enable telemetry, keeping
a backup copy of every file it modifies; (b) installs a hook invoked by
those tools; (c) runs keld-agent as a per-user background service that
listens only on your computer's loopback interface, protected by a
per-user secret; and (d) stores credentials and working files under your
user profile (by default "~/.keld") with user-only permissions. The
"keld signal uninstall" command removes the configuration the Software
added.
